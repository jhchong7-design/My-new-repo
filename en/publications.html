📕 Books (저서)

2022
Monograph

300 Years of the Korean Bible: A History of Translation and Interpretation

A groundbreaking work tracing the hidden 300-year history (1600–1900) of how the Bible was translated, interpreted, and received in Korea — revealing that Koreans read Scripture before missionaries ever arrived. Keimyung University Press, 528 pages.

Book
Historical Study

History of Israel

A systematic academic treatment of ancient Israelite history based on Old Testament texts and archaeological evidence, spanning from the patriarchal period to the Babylonian exile.

Book
Contextual Theology

Slave Liberation, Debt Cancellation, Jubilee: Korean Biblical Interpretation

An original study connecting the Old Testament Jubilee concept with Korea's own history of slave emancipation and debt forgiveness — opening new horizons for contextual Bible reading.

Book
Science & Faith

Dialogue between Scripture and Science

An exploration of the relationship between the Bible and modern science, seeking harmony between faith and reason beyond the creation-evolution debate.

Book
Introduction

Old Testament Introduction

A comprehensive introduction to each book of the Old Testament, covering background, structure, and theological themes.

Book
Faith & Life

A Meaningful Life: Modern Society and Christianity

Exploring how Christian faith addresses the existential challenges of contemporary society.

Book
Leviticus Study

Leviticus: A Place of Encounter and Sharing

An interpretation of Leviticus as a theology of divine-human encounter and communal sharing.

Book
Religion Study

Israelite Religion and the Temple

An in-depth analysis of Israel's religious traditions and the role of the Jerusalem Temple.

Book
Prophetic Study

Israel's International Relations and the Prophets

Analysis of the prophets' role and message within the international political landscape of ancient Israel.

Journal Article

"Were There Yahwistic Sanctuaries in Babylon?"

Published in the Asia Journal of Theology. A scholarly investigation into whether formal Yahwistic worship spaces existed during the Babylonian exile — an important contribution to the study of exilic Israelite religion.

Paper
